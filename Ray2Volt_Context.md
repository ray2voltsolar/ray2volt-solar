## 6. Policy And Market Context

Residential solar demand is strongly influenced by PM Surya Ghar subsidy and loan availability. Official MNRE guidelines for PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana, Central Financial Assistance to Residential Consumers, state that the implementation period of the scheme is till March 31, 2027.

As of July 9, 2026, March 31, 2027 is approximately 265 days away.

**Planning implication:**

Ray2Volt should not build the sales motion only around residential on-grid subsidy-driven demand. Residential customers remain important, but the playbook should reduce over-dependence on customers whose decision depends mainly on subsidy or loan availability.

Commercial customers can create a more durable pipeline because their buying logic is usually based on power cost reduction, ROI, tax/accounting treatment, operational reliability, and long-term electricity price protection.

**DCR / NDCR commercial and industrial impact:**

MNRE's ALMM framework includes List-I for solar PV modules and List-II for solar PV cells. MNRE's ALMM page states that only models and manufacturers included in ALMM List-I are eligible for Government Projects, Government-assisted Projects, Projects under Government Schemes and Programmes, Open Access, and Net-Metering Projects installed in India. The June 2026 ALMM List-II compliance shift means Ray2Volt should treat NDCR panels as unavailable for new commercial and industrial projects requiring net-metering / open-access / relevant compliance.

Ray2Volt operating rule: from June 5, 2026, commercial and industrial quotations should be prepared using DCR solar panels unless a specific project has a verified legal / regulatory exemption.

Cost implication: DCR solar panels are typically costlier than NDCR panels. For planning and quotation awareness, assume a DCR premium of approximately Rs. 9-12 per Wp, depending on brand, which is approximately Rs. 9,000-12,000 per kWp. Final pricing should always be checked against current procurement rates and the live package-pricing tool.

## 12. Customer Objections And Market Risks

**Key objections and risks:**

- Business loan issues due to poor CIBIL scores, weak credit profiles, or bank eligibility problems.
- High competition from companies giving very low quotes that are not feasible for Ray2Volt and may not be feasible for the competitor either.
- Inaccurate low quotations from inexperienced companies, creating customer confusion and price anchoring against unrealistic numbers.
- Competitor quotes may still be anchored to old NDCR panel pricing, even where DCR panels are now required for commercial and industrial compliance.

**Response to low competitor quotes:**

Ray2Volt should not immediately match very low competitor quotes. The salesperson should ask for the competitor quote details and compare scope line by line.

Comparison points:

- System size and expected generation.
- Panel brand, panel type, and warranty.
- Inverter brand, inverter type, and warranty.
- Structure material, structure height, and wind/load suitability.
- Wiring length and cable quality.
- Earthing, lightning protection, DCDB, ACDB, and safety devices.
- Net-metering, documentation, subsidy, and loan support.
- Installation workmanship.
- After-sales service responsibility.
- Hidden extras that may be charged later.

Core message: "We will help you compare apples to apples. If their quote includes the same quality, same scope, and same service responsibility, we will tell you honestly. But many low quotes miss important items that become extra cost or service problems later."

**Response to business loan issues:**

Ray2Volt should qualify loan eligibility early for commercial customers before investing too much sales effort.

If the customer has poor CIBIL, weak banking records, or unclear repayment capacity, the salesperson should shift the conversation toward:

- Self-funding.
- Phased installation.
- Smaller starting capacity with future expansion.
- Hybrid or off-grid options only when the customer has clear budget and a strong operational need.

The sales process should include a finance-readiness checklist to quickly identify customers who are unlikely to close through loans.

## 13. Lead-Source Context

**Current lead sources:**

- Approximately 90% of current leads come from solar consultants, freelancers, and commission-based agents.
- The remaining leads come from digital marketing and referrals.

**Lead-source risk:**

Ray2Volt is currently heavily dependent on external consultants, freelancers, and commission-based agents. This channel is expensive, unpredictable, and hard to manage.

**Top lead-generation insight:**

The main business bottleneck is lead generation. The Managing Director is currently a major bottleneck for lead generation, while the team handles leads well after they are generated.

**Top 90-day business priority:**

Create stronger and more diverse sales channels so lead generation does not depend mainly on the Managing Director or on a narrow set of unpredictable external agents.

The business priority is to increase lead flow through multiple channels while preserving the team's strong post-lead handling capability.

## 14. Planned Sales-Channel Portfolio

Ray2Volt plans to build a wider lead-source mix instead of depending mainly on solar consultants and freelancers.

**Planned channels:**

- Channel Partner Program: electrical EPC companies, electricians, contractors, and related local trade partners.
- In-house sales employees.
- Digital marketing: Google Ads and Meta Ads.
- Physical marketing: banners, door-to-door campaigns, canopy campaigns, and local visibility campaigns.
- Solar consultants: real estate agents, insurance agents, and similar commission-based local connectors.
- Customer Referral Program.

**Lead-source prioritization criteria:**

When deciding which channels to build first, Ray2Volt should prioritize:

1. Low capex.
2. Easy management.
3. Predictability.

**Lead-source rollout priority:**

1. Channel Partner Program and Solar Consultant Program first. These need formal program design, partner onboarding, commission rules, lead tracking, and management discipline.
2. Customer Referral Program can be activated immediately for existing customers because it requires very little setup time.
3. Digital marketing should follow after the partner and referral foundations are in place, so paid lead spend is supported by clear qualification, follow-up, and conversion processes.

**Partner and consultant commission trigger:**

Ray2Volt should pay Channel Partner and Solar Consultant commissions only after payment is received from the customer and the project is confirmed.

This avoids paying for low-quality leads, fake inquiries, unqualified prospects, and deals that fail due to CIBIL, loan, or budget issues.

Detailed commission pricing, slabs, and funnel-specific operating rules are intentionally out of scope for this context document. They should be decided later while designing the individual sales funnels and partner programs.

## 19. Site-Specific Add-Ons And Installation Terms

The pricing page lists add-ons that must be considered during site survey, quotation, and closing:

- Battery storage expansion is billed based on actual additional batteries, racks, and heavy-duty DC interconnect cables.
- Standard structure height is 5 ft on the south side and 7 ft on the north side. Extra height adds Rs. 1,000 per additional foot.
- Standard installation includes up to 100 meters of total AC + DC wiring. Extra wiring adds Rs. 5,000 per 100 meters.
- Extra heavy-duty battery cables beyond standard inverter-to-battery placement are billed at actuals.
- Concrete footings add Rs. 1,000 per footing.
- On-site welding, when required, adds Rs. 2,000.
- Fully custom structures such as heavy-steel carports, pergolas, and elevated sheds require custom quotes after site review.
